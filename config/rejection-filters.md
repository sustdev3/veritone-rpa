# Rejection Filters

## General Filters (all roles)

- Candidate does not appear to have had a job in the last 6 months.
- Candidate does not appear to have worked in a similar role within the last three years.
- Availability based on current role in relation to the working hours of the job.
  E.g. if someone appears to be a full-time student, he/she won't be suited to the role.
- If unsure or have any uncertainty, DO NOT reject the candidate

## Labouring Role Filters

If the role is a labouring role, also apply:

- **Age:** Anyone over 50 should be rejected. If the age of the candidate is not shown,
  infer from their email address. E.g. fred1967@yahoo or melanie75@gmail were probably
  born in 1967 and 1975 respectively.
- **Sex:** Females will almost always be rejected unless there is clear evidence of recent
  labouring experience.

### Heavy Labouring Role Filters

If it is a heavy labouring role, also apply:

- **Weight:** Generally looking for someone who weighs at least 80 to 90 kg.
- **Nationality:** E.g. someone who is a Pacific Islander (e.g. Samoan) can be expected
  to be strong.
- **Sex:** Cannot be female.

## Employment Date Clarity Filter (high-volume only)

If the number of candidates after initial filtering (location and keyword) is more than 60,
look for clear employment dates. E.g. if someone just puts a year against a particular role
(e.g. 2024) it is hard to trust — it could mean the person was employed for anywhere from
1 day to most of the year. Candidates will often do this to create the illusion of a long
period of work.
